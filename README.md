*A product strategy and market analysis case study developed during the Slalom Case Competition.*

> **Competition:** Slalom Case Competition  
> **Client:** FlyUrban (Case Study)  
> **Industry:** Urban Air Mobility (UAM) / Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL)  
> **Outcome:** 🏆 2nd Runner-Up (3rd Place out of 20 Teams)

# Executive Summary

As part of the Slalom Case Competition, my team developed a market entry strategy for **FlyUrban**, a Seattle-based startup focused on electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL) aircraft.

The objective was to evaluate FlyUrban's readiness to enter the emerging Urban Air Mobility (UAM) market by analyzing regulatory challenges, consumer adoption, infrastructure requirements, and competitive positioning.

Using market research, business analysis, and strategic frameworks, our team developed recommendations aimed at supporting FlyUrban's long-term growth and commercialization strategy.

Our solution earned **2nd Runner-Up (3rd Place)** among **20 competing teams**.

# Challenge

Urban Air Mobility has the potential to redefine transportation by reducing congestion and enabling faster, more sustainable travel. However, commercial adoption depends on far more than innovative technology.

FlyUrban faced several critical questions:

- How will evolving regulations impact commercialization?
- Will consumers trust and adopt eVTOL transportation?
- What infrastructure is required to support operations?
- How can FlyUrban differentiate itself in an increasingly competitive market?

Our challenge was to evaluate these uncertainties and recommend a practical strategy for market entry.

# Objectives

The project focused on four primary areas of analysis.

### Regulatory Research

Analyzed current and emerging regulations affecting the Urban Air Mobility industry to understand how future policy changes could influence FlyUrban's business strategy and commercial expansion.

### Consumer Sentiment

Evaluated publicly available research and consumer data to identify potential adoption barriers, customer concerns, and opportunities within the emerging eVTOL market.

### Infrastructure Assessment

Researched the operational infrastructure required to support commercial eVTOL services, including vertiports, charging infrastructure, maintenance requirements, and estimated implementation costs.

### Competitive Positioning

Conducted market research on competing transportation solutions and emerging Urban Air Mobility companies before developing a SWOT analysis to evaluate FlyUrban's competitive position.

# Key Findings

## Regulation Will Shape Commercial Success

While eVTOL technology continues to mature, regulatory approval remains one of the largest barriers to commercialization.

A phased rollout strategy would allow FlyUrban to validate operations while adapting to evolving FAA regulations.


## Consumer Trust Is Essential

Although Urban Air Mobility offers significant benefits, adoption depends heavily on public confidence in safety, affordability, and reliability.

Building trust through pilot programs and transparent communication would be essential during the early stages of commercialization.


## Infrastructure Represents a Major Investment

Commercial eVTOL operations require significant supporting infrastructure, including vertiports, charging stations, maintenance facilities, and air traffic coordination.

Strategic partnerships with municipalities and airports could reduce implementation costs while accelerating deployment.


## Competitive Advantage Extends Beyond Technology

As more competitors enter the Urban Air Mobility market, long-term differentiation will depend not only on aircraft technology but also on customer experience, operational efficiency, strategic partnerships, and scalable business models.


# Strategic Recommendations

Based on our research, our team recommended that FlyUrban:

- Prioritize regulatory readiness before large-scale expansion.
- Launch pilot programs to validate customer demand.
- Establish partnerships with municipalities and infrastructure providers.
- Focus initial operations on premium commuter routes.
- Expand incrementally based on operational data and customer adoption.
